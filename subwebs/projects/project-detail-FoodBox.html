Foodbox.to
A Mutual-Aid Initiative in Toronto

Core Issue

The Foodbox is a public food pantry and mutual aid initiative conceptualized and built by me with the help of the Impact Skateboard Club Youth Council. The idea of making pantries came to me after visiting a homeless encampment at Trinity Bellwoods Park in 2021 along with the Youth Council. As we handed out care-packages I got to know the residents and realized the severity of the financial stress from the pandemic. We agreed that a food pantry would benefit the community.

I built the pantries with wooden 2x4's and plywood, and decorated them with hand-painted vegetables on skateboards! The Youth Council created an Instagram to promote them (@foodbox.to), and they were generously hosted by several restaurants for the coming years.

People regularly donated non-perishables which were then taken by people in need, and the cycle continued. Seeing how something I built allowed people to help others or be helped was a powerful feeling. The FoodBox was the first big woodworking project I had ever made, and it taught me a lot about furniture building, which I do a lot of now.
